McDonald's (June 2020-June 2023)
I got my work certificate and started working the minute I could. I remember Summer of 2020, I just got my work certificate and got a job at McDonald's, making $7.25 an hour, minimum wage. While $7.25 isn't much, especially today, it felt like all of the money in the world to 14 year old me. I was in disbelief that I finally had my own money to spend, and I didn't have to spend my parents' money. The day I got my first check: $403.52, I knew I was going to do whatever it took to make it and fight for my independence. When I finished working at McDonald's I was making $13.00 an hour, and I was proficient in every scene: Cooking, Drive Thru, Janitorial- I could practically run the store on my own- and McDonald's helped very much financially during school.

Walmart (May 2023-November 2023)
I started working at Walmart in Summer of 2023, and having a job outside of fast food was life changing. I finally felt like I had time to myself and I didn't have to always be ready to come in to work when they needed me. Walmart has helped me throughout my mental health journey- providing time off, therapy, and on top of that, they're giving me $15 an hour, which is the most I've made through and through. No matter what, I am eternally greatful for the people who worked with me, since some days they were the only people who check in with me and ask me how I'm doing. Though short lived, Walmart was one of my favorite jobs.
